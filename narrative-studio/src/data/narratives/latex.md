# Mathematical Foundations

## Inline Mathematics

The quadratic formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ is one of the most fundamental equations in algebra.

Similarly, Euler's identity $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$ is often called the most beautiful equation in mathematics because it relates five fundamental constants.

The derivative of a function can be expressed as $f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$.

The Fourier transform is defined as:

$$
\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) e^{-2\pi i x \xi} dx
$$

## Display Mathematics (Bracket Notation)

Maxwell's equations in differential form:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} &= \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} \\
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} &= 0 \\
\nabla \times \mathbf{E} &= -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \\
\nabla \times \mathbf{B} &= \mu_0\mathbf{J} + \mu_0\epsilon_0\frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}
\end{aligned}
\]

The probability density function of a normal distribution:

\[
f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^2}
\]

## Matrix Notation

The identity matrix in $n$ dimensions:

$$
I_n = \begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 1 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

## Complex Expressions

The partition function in statistical mechanics:

$$
Z = \sum_{i} e^{-\beta E_i} = \text{Tr}(e^{-\beta \hat{H}})
$$

Where $\beta = \frac{1}{k_B T}$ is the inverse temperature.
